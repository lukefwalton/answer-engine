On Amos the herdsman of Tekoa: justice is not a ledger the strong keep against
the weak, but the weight of heaven set on the side of the wronged. The plumb-line
is held to the wall of the nation and the wall is found to lean. Where the courts
sell the righteous for silver and the needy for a pair of shoes, the worship that
continues above that wrong is itself the offence; the feast is noise until the
judgement runs down like waters. The mercy is in the warning: that the line is
shown at all, and shown in time, is the patience of God toward a people still
able to return.
